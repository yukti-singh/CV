The whole family dancing on marriage

If you're a couple and just want to get a 3-4 minute dance performance prepared with the help of our expert wedding choreographers, call us.
If you want a BIG INDIAN FAMILY DANCE, create a list of all family members – cousins, uncles, aunties, grand parents etc who will be coming to the event – you will need this list for invitations anyway, someone may have already made it.
Put a tick against every person's name whose dancing on your event would make you really happy.
Call those people and ask if they'd be willing to dance. Do tell them that it is a time commitment and that they will need to spare a minimum of 5 hours on 5 different days to be able to participate.
Some people are shy, don't force them, but once we begin practice, invite them to watch a session. Some people think it's very hard and say no at first but then join in when they see others performing.
Once your final list is complete, give us a call as we will ask you how many people are participating in the family choreography.
You can also make a list of your favourite songs and try to think of the groups of people and which songs they'll be dancing to. Once we meet, we'll give you some ideas on songs, but it's good to have thought about it once before we meet.
